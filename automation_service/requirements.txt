## 1. Decide the scope (this is critical)

First, define **what your database will and will not do**.

### Common models

**A. Product lookup database (most common)**

* Input: UPC / EAN / GTIN
* Output: product name, brand, category, image, description
* Examples: Go-UPC, Barcode Spider

**B. Verification database**

* Input: barcode number
* Output: valid/invalid, GS1 prefix, issuing country
* No product images or descriptions

**C. Regional or niche database**

* Example: *Jamaica-only products*, Caribbean products, local manufacturers
* Much easier to start and grow

üëâ **Recommendation:** Start with **C**, then expand.

## 2. Understand the legal reality (important)

* **GS1 owns the standard, not the data**
* You **cannot issue ‚Äúofficial‚Äù GTINs** unless you are GS1
* You **can store, index, and display barcode ‚Üí product data**

## 5. Barcode validation logic (must-have)

Even without product data, you should:

* Validate **check digits** (EAN-13, UPC-A)
* Identify **barcode type**
* Identify **GS1 prefix (country/region)**

This builds trust and credibility.
